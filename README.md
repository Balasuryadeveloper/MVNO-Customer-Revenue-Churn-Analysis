# 📊 MVNO Customer Revenue & Churn Analysis

---

## 📌 Problem Statement

In the telecom industry, especially in MVNO (Mobile Virtual Network Operator) businesses, customer churn is a major challenge that directly impacts revenue growth and long-term sustainability.

Customers frequently switch providers due to factors such as pricing, service usage, plan benefits, and overall experience. Identifying and understanding the key drivers behind churn is critical for improving customer retention and maximizing revenue.

This project aims to analyze customer data to uncover patterns of churn, revenue contribution, and customer behavior across different plans, regions, and usage patterns.

---

## 🎯 Objective

The primary objectives of this project are:

- 🔍 Analyze customer churn patterns in the MVNO domain  
- 💰 Evaluate revenue contribution and identify revenue loss due to churn  
- 📈 Calculate key telecom KPIs such as Churn Rate, ARPU, and Revenue  
- 📊 Understand customer behavior based on:
  - Usage (Data, Calls, SMS)
  - Pricing
  - Plan type (Prepaid vs Postpaid)
  - Region
  - Tenure  
- 🚨 Identify high-risk customer segments and high-value customer churn  
- 💡 Provide actionable business insights for improving customer retention  

---

## 📂 Dataset Overview

The project uses multiple datasets:

- **Customer Data** → Customer demographics, region, join date, status  
- **Plan Data** → Plan name, type (Prepaid/Postpaid)  
- **Usage Data** → Data usage, call minutes, SMS usage  
- **Billing Data** → Monthly charges, total revenue, payment status  

---

## 📊 Key KPIs

- 📉 **Churn Rate**
- 💰 **Total Revenue**
- 📉 **Revenue Loss (Churn Impact)**
- 📲 **Average Usage (Data, Calls, SMS)**
- 💵 **ARPU (Average Revenue Per User)**

---

## 🧠 Key Insights

- 🔥 Churn rate is significantly high, impacting business revenue  
- 📉 Low-usage customers are more likely to churn  
- 💸 Pricing plays a key role in customer retention  
- 👑 High-value customers also show churn risk  
- ⏳ New customers (low tenure) are more likely to churn  
- 🌍 Customer behavior varies significantly by region  
- 📊 Prepaid users show higher churn compared to postpaid users  
- 📉 Some plans show low engagement, indicating poor plan design

## ✅ Conclusion

The analysis reveals that customer churn is driven by multiple factors, including usage patterns, pricing sensitivity, tenure, and regional differences.

Low engagement customers and newly onboarded users are at higher risk of churn, while high-value customers contribute significantly to revenue loss if not retained.

To improve retention, telecom companies should focus on:
- Enhancing engagement for low-usage users  
- Optimizing pricing strategies  
- Targeting early-stage customers  
- Providing special retention offers for high-value customers
